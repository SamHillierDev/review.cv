# Understanding Results

After uploading your CV, ReviewCV provides a detailed analysis. Here's how to interpret each section.

## Overall Score

Your CV receives an overall score from 0 to 100. This is a weighted average of all category scores.

| Score Range | Rating |
|------------|--------|
| 80–100 | Excellent |
| 60–79 | Good |
| 40–59 | Needs Improvement |
| 0–39 | Poor |

## Category Breakdown

### Formatting & Structure

Evaluates layout consistency, section organisation, and visual hierarchy. Looks for:

- Consistent heading styles
- Logical section ordering (contact info, experience, education, skills)
- Appropriate use of whitespace
- Readable font choices and sizing

### Content Quality

Assesses the strength of your descriptions and bullet points:

- Action verbs at the start of bullet points
- Quantified achievements (numbers, percentages, metrics)
- Relevance of included information
- Spelling and grammar

### ATS Compatibility

Checks whether your CV will parse correctly through Applicant Tracking Systems:

- Standard section headings
- No text embedded in images or graphics
- Compatible file format
- Clean, parseable structure

## Suggestions

Each suggestion includes:

- **What to fix** — A description of the issue
- **Why it matters** — How this impacts your CV's effectiveness
- **Priority** — High, medium, or low impact

Focus on high-priority suggestions first for the biggest improvement to your score.
